Human Development Index Measures in the Top Five Most Populous Countries

By Madison Hindo

These charts are both depict measures of the Human Development Index in the five most populous countries in the world. The Human Development Index is a measurement that encompasses education, health and standards of living. The first chart illustrates changes in HDI from 2010 to 2014, and it shows that the HDI level has increased for each country for the time period shown. The second chart splits up the HDI by gender and shows gaps in the measure between males and females. The largest gender gap can be seen in India, and the smallest is in Brazil.
